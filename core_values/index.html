Justice

"He has told you, O mortal, what is good; and what does the Lord require of you but to do justice, to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?" Micah 6:8

We make decisions and support systems which promote the God-given dignity of humanity. We are involved with the Whizkids tutoring program to invest in local students, we partner with, and regularly visit, a coffee-growing village in Guatemala to support that community, and our Faith and Justice team connects us to important initiatives locally and globally.

Arts

"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." Genesis 1:1

We celebrate the beauty of God's creation, and the talents God gives his people reflected through the Arts.

Our services reflect the creative gifts of our community and we are blessed with many members who work and study in the Arts. In addition, Rohs St Cafe has become a well-known local music and arts venue.

Relationships

"'Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your strength and with all your mind' and 'Love your neighbor as yourself.'" Luke 10:27

We work towards healthy, growing relationships, understanding that God created us for community and we strive to express this value through our investment in our cafe, small groups and international ministry.

Spirituality

"Where can I go from your Spirit? Where can I flee from your presence? If I go up to the heavens, you are there; if I make my bed in the depths, you are there." Psalm 139:7,8
"The obviously well kept secret of the 'ordinary' is that it is made to be a receptacle of the divine, a place where the life of God flows. But the divine is not pushy." Dallas Willard

We walk with God as individuals and as a community, remembering that God is the reason for all we do and the source of all we need in order to do it. Our small groups, Sunday teaching and campus ministry activities work towards this value. We also endeavor to find God's presence in everything we do, every conversation we have, every cup of coffee we brew.

Our mission is simple, to share the love of Jesus in Uptown Cincinnati and to develop lifelong Kingdom leaders.

What We Believe

We believe in the centrality of Jesus Christ as the way to a restored relationship with God through his perfect life, redemptive death, and bodily resurrection. We ascribe to Jesus' words that Jesus is the way, the truth and the life, and none come to God except through him (John 14:6).

We are very committed to the foundation of the Bible in what we preach, teach, and study. We believe the Bible is the inspired word of God, and as Paul writes,

"All scripture is inspired by God and is useful for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, so that everyone who belongs to God may be proficient, equipped for every good work." 2 Timothy 3:16-17

Regarding the role of the Christ-follower in today's world, we try to be very intentional in helping further God's Kingdom here on earth through evangelism, global missions and through working for Biblical Justice.

Most of what takes place at UCC is a result of the creativity and passion of friends who have decided to make something happen. We recognize that every person in the church has unique gifts, given to them by God to contribute and our goal is to help people develop their skills and passions.

Please join us in the rewarding work of shared ministry.

Engagement with the Poor through City Gospel Mission

The Outlet: On the first Saturday of every month, we go to the Outlet to engage with Cincinnati's homeless and impoverished. The Outlet is a ministry of City Gospel Mission that provides a healthy environment for low-income people to develop positive relationships with volunteers and to have healthy conversations over breakfast, coffee, board games, crafts, pool and ping pong. We meet at the church at 8:15am and carpool downtown.

Whiz Kids Tutoring: We partner with City Gospel Mission's Whiz Kids program to tutor kids at a local school. Whiz Kids is a literacy-based, one-to-one tutoring and mentoring program that reaches over 1,300 at-risk youth throughout Greater Cincinnati.

To learn more about the Outlet or Whiz Kids, or call 513.241.2140.

Guatemala Mission

Our church and cafe have a close relationship with coffee farmers in the Guatemalan village of Santa Maria de Jesus. Several graduates from UCC have worked there long-term and we also take a team each June to work with indigenous leaders there. To get involved, or call 513.241.2140.

Rohs Street Cafe

Our not-for-profit cafe provides a community space for gathering and supports coffee farmers in Guatemala through our connections with La Armonia Hermosa, a coffee co-op. Want to volunteer? Serve coffee? Help with marketing?
